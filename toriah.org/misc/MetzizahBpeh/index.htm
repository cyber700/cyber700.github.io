The Metziza B'peh Controversy

A recent article in Pediatrics (2004) authored by eleven physicians and one individual with a PhD (Rabbi Moshe D. Tendler), attempted to connect metzitzah b’peh and neonatal  (infant) herpes. The article attempted to establish a connection between eight infants who experienced an outbreak of herpes on the milah area shortly after their bris which was done by a mohel who performed metzitzah b’peh. This article assigned blame based on speculation rather than established scientific procedure and it is difficult to understand how it passed peer review (see article by Dr. Berman below).

In 2005, a respected New York mohel was charged with transmitting a virus to three infants. In mid-October 2004, the mohel performed the brisos of twin infants in Brooklyn, N.Y. Ten days later, one infant died from herpes and the other tested positive for the virus. A few weeks later, the New York City Health Department found a third infant in Staten Island who also tested positive for herpes after being circumcised by this mohel in late 2003. The health department has released its side of the story to the mainstream media, pinning responsibility for the transmission on the mohel. The mohel’s version of the story differs and vindicates him from the blame. The NY city health department, although claiming to have proof of their claims, has not produced any evidence corroborating their version. At the state level, after a full investigation of the issue, the Health department rescinded its injunction against the mohel and appointed the Hisachdus Harabanim (CRC) as the oversight committee.
